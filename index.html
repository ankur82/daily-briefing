Key watch points — Monday 4 May 2026
1
Operation Sindoor — India strikes Pakistan; ceasefire calm triggers relief gap-up
India's military conducted targeted strikes on nine terror infrastructure sites in Pakistan and PoK in response to the April 22 Pahalgam terror attack. Border areas were "largely peaceful" over the weekend, sparking a relief rally. India VIX spiked 18.5% last week on geopolitical fear; markets open with a gap-up as ceasefire signals hold. Historically, Nifty has delivered positive 1-month returns after India-Pakistan incidents (+16.5% post-Kargil, +6.3% post-Pulwama/Balakot). Key sectors: Defence (HAL, BEL, BHEL, Cochin Shipyard) outperforming; tourism, hotels, and aviation remain pressured. Pakistan's KSE-30 dropped 6–7% on escalation fears.
2
IMD forecasts below-normal monsoon 2026 — 92% LPA, El Niño returning
IMD's advance Southwest Monsoon forecast signals 92% of Long Period Average — the first below-normal season in three years, driven by potential El Niño development mid-season. This is the single biggest domestic macro risk for FY27: below-normal rains reignite food inflation, narrow the RBI rate-cut window, and weaken rural consumption. Sectors at risk: FMCG (volume slowdown), agri-inputs, two-wheelers, and microfinance lenders. Onset for Kerala remains on track for early June. Watch this week for IMD's state-wise breakdown — it will be the first mover for weather-sensitive stocks this season.
3
Brent slides to $108 — partial Hormuz premium unwind; India gets CAD relief
Brent crude pulled back 2% to $108.17 from the $111 Hormuz peak as diplomatic signals from the Iran-US channel provide marginal relief. Still 59% above pre-conflict levels ($68), but each $3 decline from peak saves India ~$2bn annualised on its import bill. If Brent sustains below $105, it meaningfully improves India's CAD trajectory and reopens the RBI rate-cut debate. OMCs (IOC, BPCL, HPCL) and aviation stocks (IndiGo, SpiceJet) to watch for a recovery trade. Geopolitical risk floor remains fragile — any re-escalation in the Strait would reverse the pullback sharply.
4
Q4 FY26 peak earnings week — banks, FMCG, IT in the spotlight
India's Q4 FY26 results season peaks this week. Key prints expected from major PSU and private sector banks (NIM trajectory, slippage ratios, credit cost guidance after the geopolitically charged quarter), FMCG leaders (volume vs. margin trade-off under high input costs), and IT companies (deal ramp-up pace, FY27 revenue guidance). Bank Nifty fell 1.63% on Apr 30 amid broader risk-off — a strong beat with improving asset quality could catalyse a sharp catch-up rally. Nifty Midcap (P/E ~35x) remains vulnerable; any earnings miss will see sharp de-rating. TCS/Infosys deal pipeline commentary is key for IT sector sentiment.
5
Gold at $4,661 — India-Pakistan bid renews safe-haven demand; +31% YTD
Gold recovered to $4,661 (+0.68%) as India-Pakistan tensions renewed safe-haven flows, partially reversing last week's pullback from the $4,654 peak. At +31% YTD, gold continues to significantly outperform all major asset classes globally. Structural drivers intact: geopolitical risk, central bank accumulation, and dollar softening trend. Domestically, MCX gold and Sovereign Gold Bond holders benefit from the geopolitical premium. Risk: a decisive ceasefire agreement or Hormuz resolution could trigger a sharp $100–150 pullback from current elevated levels — monitor de-escalation signals closely.
