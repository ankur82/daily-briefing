Executive Summary
Positive Close
Markets rebounded sharply. Nifty closed at 24,093 (+0.81%) and Sensex at 77,304 (+0.83%), reversing Friday's losses. The catalyst: Iran's new peace proposal on the Strait of Hormuz lifted risk appetite across Asia. Broader markets outperformed benchmarks. IT was the standout laggard (−5%), while Defence PSUs surged on India-Pakistan geopolitical interest.
Iran peace proposal lifted markets: Iran submitted a new proposal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, sparking Asia-wide risk-on sentiment. Nikkei +1.41%, Kospi +1.9%, S&P 500 hit a new record (+0.8%) on Friday.
IT sector hammered −5%: Top losers — Infosys, HCL Tech, TCS, Tech Mahindra, Sun Pharma. Global demand uncertainty and valuation concerns weighed heavily on tech exports.
Defence PSUs rallied +2.65%: HAL, BEL, Bharat Dynamics, GRSE saw strong buying as India-Pakistan border tensions kept defence spending expectations elevated.
Crude still elevated at $107.66: Despite the Hormuz proposal, talks stalled during the day. Brent +2.2%. Rupee under pressure near ₹93, weighing on OMCs and import-heavy sectors.
Q4 earnings in focus: Coal India, UltraTech Cement, Varun Beverages among 48 companies reporting today. Maruti (Apr 28), Kotak & Bajaj Finance (May 2) are the key upcoming events.
