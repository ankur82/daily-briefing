Key watch points today
1
Brent at $114.60 — Hormuz blockade escalates sharply
US forces were ordered to target Iranian vessels in the Strait of Hormuz after latest talks collapsed. UAE confirmed its May OPEC exit. Brent +68% vs pre-war $68. Every $10 rise in Brent adds ~50bps to India's CAD. A genuine ceasefire could cut crude $15–20 within days; further escalation raises India's stagflation risk materially.
2
Alphabet & Amazon earnings beat — US futures higher overnight
Both Mag 7 giants crushed Q1 estimates after Wednesday's bell. Google Cloud growth reassured AI investors; Amazon's AWS revenue exceeded street expectations. Meta fell on outsized AI capex commitments. S&P 500 futures are up ~0.6% pre-India open — providing a supportive backdrop even as oil clouds the macro picture.
3
India Q4 FY26 earnings — banks lead today's rally
Bank Nifty +0.85% as private lenders report healthy NIMs and controlled slippages. Q4 results season is entering its peak week. Analysts are closely watching gross margin commentary across FMCG and industrials given elevated input costs. Nifty midcap remains muted as small/mid-cap valuations (P/E ~36.3x) draw caution from FIIs.
4
Fed higher-for-longer: US 10Y at 4.39% — RBI on hold
US 10-year yield hit its highest since March — sticky core inflation and oil above $100 have effectively removed all 2026 Fed cuts from the base case. USD/INR at 94.86 (near YTD high, +14% y/y) is squeezing importers. RBI repo rate stays at 5.25%; persistent crude >$100 has eliminated the near-term rate-cut window entirely.
5
IMD pre-season monsoon forecast — single biggest domestic catalyst
IMD's pre-season monsoon forecast is due this week and is the most important domestic macro event of the month. A below-normal forecast would reignite food inflation fears, widen the fiscal deficit outlook, and shut the rate-cut window for all of H1 FY27. Markets will react sharply — watch rural consumption and agri-input stocks as the first movers.
