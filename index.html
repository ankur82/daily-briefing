Key watch points today
1
US-Iran war: UAE exits OPEC & Hormuz risk premium
The war completes its second month. UAE signalled it will exit OPEC in May — a seismic geopolitical realignment. Brent at $111/bbl. A genuine Hormuz deal could slash Brent $15–20; further escalation deepens India's stagflation risk via elevated CAD and imported inflation.
2
US megacap earnings — Meta reports today
Meta reports after US market close today; Apple, Amazon, and others follow later this week. These 5 names account for ~25% of S&P 500 market cap. Watch AI capex guidance and any cost-pressure commentary from elevated energy/logistics — it will drive near-term US and global sentiment.
3
India Q4 FY26 earnings season & Nifty recovery
Nifty is up ~238 pts today, reclaiming 24,200. Q4 results continue — sector mix matters. Brokerages have cut Nifty targets citing oil risks; a selective approach is favoured, with Goldman Sachs preferring financials, staples, and telecom over energy-sensitive sectors.
4
INR pressure & Fed higher-for-longer
USD/INR at 94.77 (+14% vs year-ago ~83) is squeezing import-heavy corporates. US 10Y at 4.35% and rising on sticky core inflation. No Fed cut expected in May or June. Persistent oil above $95 has priced out most 2026 cuts; further INR weakness could force RBI to tighten liquidity.
5
IMD monsoon forecast & food inflation watch
RBI repo on hold at 5.25%; rate-cut window has narrowed. IMD pre-season monsoon forecast is imminent — this is the single most important domestic macro signal of the week. A below-normal forecast would re-ignite food price fears and close the rate-cut window entirely for H1 FY27.
